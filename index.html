Tourism Overview by State

Australia has a history of being a tourism goldmine for travellers worldwide. The lucky country offers an abundance of adventures for all types of tourists. Whether you prefer tropical places, surfing hotspots or gastronomically-obsessed cities, Australia has a place to visit. Use this map to explore distribution of tourists amongst its main states. Unfortunately, Tasmania, you have been left out.

State Tourism Growth

The following bump chart ranks the mainland Australian states on their rate of tourism growth over the years. Press on a state's colour on the legend, to get a zoomed-in, filtered view of the area's trajectory with its tourism figures.

Popular Regions

This visual lists top 5 most popular regions for a particular Australian state. Use the selection pane to filter for a different state, and hover over the bars to view number of tourists visited. As the graph shows, a state's main urban hub namely its capital city ranks as the most visited regional area. This trend is skewed by the fact business trips are reflected as tourist trips under Tourism Australia. Indeed, larger cities receive greater business tourism compared to a regional area focused on relaxed holidaying, such as Far North Queensland.

Reasons for Tourism

The following chart groups the number of tourism trips for each state based on business, holiday, visiting, and other. Holidaying remains the most common category of tourism for each state.

Tourism across Australian Seasons

Unsurprisingly, the summer months remains the most popular season for like-minded tourists. Overall, tourism numbers have increased Australia-wide registering a 30% increase in yearly tourism numbers in 2016 compared to the beginning of the dataset (2010)
